# Problem Statement

**Track:** Track 2 — Agentic Compliance: From Regulatory Text to Operational Action

---

## SEBI's Framing

SEBI's Track 2 brief centers on two challenges:

### Part A — Dynamic Regulatory Translation
Interpreting a new or amended regulatory requirement, mapping it to the affected intermediary's operational processes, and updating compliance workflows in a timely and consistent manner. Today this depends heavily on manual legal interpretation — resulting in uneven implementation, delayed adaptation, and divergent interpretations across similarly situated intermediaries.

### Part B — Regulation as Machine-Actionable Logic
SEBI calls for **programmable, auditable compliance logic**, rather than human-readable text alone.

---

## The Problem in Practice

A compliance officer at a stock brokerage faces this every month:

1. SEBI releases a new circular — sometimes 30 pages, sometimes 150+.
2. Some circulars modify previous circulars, some replace older rules entirely, and some change a single sentence buried on page 40.
3. The team must read the entire circular, compare it against everything issued before, and manually determine what actually changed.
4. They must identify which departments are affected, update internal SOPs, assign work, track implementation, and collect audit evidence.
5. This can take **days to weeks** — and different people often interpret the same regulation differently, creating inconsistent compliance and real penalty risk.

> This is a recurring, expensive, high-stakes operational bottleneck inside every regulated intermediary, every reporting cycle — not a hypothetical inconvenience.

## Who Is Affected

| Persona | What They Need |
|---|---|
| **Compliance Officer** | Know exactly what changed in a new circular and what to do about it — fast |
| **Compliance Manager** | A consistent read across the team, so officers don't interpret the same circular differently |
| **Internal Auditor** | A citation-backed record of why an obligation was retired or changed |
| **Risk Team** | Early visibility into newly added or tightened obligations |
| **SEBI Inspector / Regulator** | Ability to check how consistently different intermediaries interpret the same circular |
